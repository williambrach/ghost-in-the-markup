Smoky Chorizo and Seafood Paella

Servings:
6-8
Prep Time:
30 minutes
Cook Time:
40 minutes

Ingredients

For the Sofrito Base

  • 2 red bell peppers, finely diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, grated
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika

For the Paella

  • 2 1/2 cups Bomba or Calasparra rice
  • 6 cups seafood stock
  • 1/2 pound Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound mussels, cleaned
  • 1/2 pound calamari rings
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 teaspoons saffron threads
  • 2 lemons, cut into wedges
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Saffron:
    • Toast saffron threads in a dry pan for 30 seconds
    • Steep in 1/4 cup hot water for 15 minutes
    • Set aside for later use
  2. Make the Sofrito:
    • Heat olive oil in a large paella pan over medium heat
    • Saut√© onions and peppers until softened (8-10 minutes)
    • Add garlic and cook for 2 minutes
    • Stir in grated tomatoes and smoked paprika
    • Cook until mixture thickens (10 minutes)
  3. Build the Paella:
    • Add chorizo to sofrito and cook until oils release
    • Stir in rice and toast for 2-3 minutes
    • Pour in saffron mixture and seafood stock
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce to simmer
    • Cook without stirring for 18-20 minutes
  4. Add Seafood:
    • Arrange shrimp, mussels, and calamari on top
    • Sprinkle with frozen peas
    • Continue cooking until seafood is done (8-10 minutes)
    • Listen for crackling sound indicating socarrat formation
  5. Finish and Serve:
    • Remove from heat and let rest for 5-10 minutes
    • Garnish with parsley and lemon wedges
    • Serve directly from the paella pan

Notes

  • Traditional paella develops a crispy bottom layer called socarrat
  • Do not stir the rice once stock is added
  • Use fresh seafood for best results
  • Bomba rice can be substituted with Arborio if unavailable
