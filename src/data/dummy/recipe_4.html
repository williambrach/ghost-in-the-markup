Moroccan Stuffed Eggplant with Jeweled Couscous

Servings:
6
Prep Time:
25 minutes
Cook Time:
50 minutes

Ingredients

For the Eggplant

  • 3 large eggplants, halved lengthwise
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons honey

For the Filling and Couscous

  • 2 cups pearl couscous
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup golden raisins
  • 1/2 cup pistachios, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ras el hanout
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3 cups vegetable stock
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • Greek yogurt for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Eggplant:
    • Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C)
    • Score eggplant flesh in a diamond pattern
    • Brush with olive oil and season with cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper
    • Place cut-side down on baking sheet
    • Roast for 30-35 minutes until tender
  2. Make the Filling:
    • Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat
    • Sauté onion, garlic, carrots, and bell pepper until soft (8-10 minutes)
    • Add ras el hanout, cinnamon, and tomato paste
    • Stir in chickpeas and cook for 5 minutes
    • Add half the dried fruit and herbs
  3. Prepare the Couscous:
    • Toast couscous in a dry pan until lightly golden
    • Add vegetable stock and bring to a boil
    • Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes
    • Fluff with fork and stir in remaining dried fruit and herbs
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste
  4. Stuff and Finish:
    • Turn eggplant halves cut-side up
    • Drizzle with honey
    • Fill with chickpea mixture
    • Return to oven for 10 minutes
    • Toast pistachios until fragrant
  5. Serve:
    • Place couscous on serving plates
    • Top with stuffed eggplant
    • Garnish with pomegranate seeds and pistachios
    • Serve with a dollop of Greek yogurt
    • Sprinkle with additional fresh herbs

Notes

  • Eggplants can be roasted ahead and reheated
  • Ras el hanout can be substituted with a mix of warm spices
  • Make it vegan by omitting honey and using plant-based yogurt
  • Couscous can be replaced with quinoa for a gluten-free option
