Golden Butternut Squash Risotto with Sage and Parmesan

Servings:
4-6
Prep Time:
20 minutes
Cook Time:
45 minutes

Ingredients

For the Squash

  • 1 medium butternut squash (about 2 lbs), peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 tablespoon honey

For the Risotto

  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken stock
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 1 small leek, finely sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • 8-10 fresh sage leaves, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 tablespoons butter (for finishing)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped chives
  • 1/2 cup roasted pumpkin seeds

Instructions

  1. Roast the Squash:
    • Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C)
    • Toss squash cubes with olive oil, salt, pepper, and thyme sprigs
    • Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and lightly caramelized
    • Remove thyme sprigs and set aside half the squash. Mash the other half into a purée
  2. Prepare the Base:
    • In a saucepan, heat stock and keep warm over low heat
    • In a large heavy-bottomed pot, melt butter over medium heat
    • Add a splash of olive oil to prevent butter from burning
    • Sauté onion until translucent (5-7 minutes)
    • Add garlic and cook for 1 minute more
  3. Make the Risotto:
    • Add rice to pot and toast for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly
    • Add wine and stir until mostly absorbed
    • Begin adding warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly
    • Wait for each addition to be almost fully absorbed before adding more
    • Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes
    • Stir in a pinch of saffron for added color and flavor
  4. Finish the Dish:
    • When rice is almost done (al dente), stir in the squash purée
    • Add a dollop of mascarpone cheese for extra creaminess
    • Add the roasted squash cubes, sage, and Parmesan
    • Finish with butter and stir until creamy
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste
    • Let rest for 2 minutes before serving
  5. Serve:
    • Spoon into warm bowls
    • Top with additional Parmesan and fresh sage leaves
    • Sprinkle with crispy bacon bits if desired
    • Drizzle with high-quality olive oil if desired

Notes

  • Rice should be creamy but still have a slight bite
  • Risotto should be loose enough to spread slowly when plated
  • Leftover squash purée can be frozen for future use
  • White wine can be substituted with additional stock if preferred
