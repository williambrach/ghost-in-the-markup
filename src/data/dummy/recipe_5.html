Spicy Korean Fried Chicken Rice Bowl

Servings:
4
Prep Time:
40 minutes
Cook Time:
35 minutes

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 2 pounds chicken thighs, boneless and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup potato starch or cornstarch
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Sauce and Bowl

  • 1/2 cup gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 4 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 2 cups kimchi, roughly chopped
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Nori sheets, cut into strips

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sauce:
    • Whisk together gochujang, honey, soy sauce, minced garlic
    • Add rice vinegar and sesame oil until well combined
    • Set aside to let flavors meld
  2. Prepare the Chicken:
    • Mix flour, potato starch, salt, and pepper in a bowl
    • Dip chicken pieces in beaten egg
    • Coat thoroughly in flour mixture
    • Let coated pieces rest for 10 minutes
  3. Fry the Chicken:
    • Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in a deep pot
    • Fry chicken in batches until golden brown (6-7 minutes)
    • Remove and drain on paper towels
    • Double fry each batch for extra crispiness (2-3 minutes)
  4. Prepare Components:
    • Cook eggs to soft-boiled stage (6.5 minutes)
    • Quickly blanch spinach and season with sesame oil and salt
    • Warm cooked rice if needed
    • Toss fried chicken pieces in prepared sauce
  5. Assemble Bowls:
    • Divide rice among four bowls
    • Arrange sauced chicken, kimchi, spinach, and cucumber
    • Place halved soft-boiled egg on top
    • Garnish with green onions, sesame seeds, and nori
    • Serve immediately while chicken is crispy

Notes

  • Double-frying the chicken ensures maximum crispiness
  • Adjust gochujang amount to control spiciness
  • Can substitute chicken thighs with wings or breast meat
  • Components can be prepared in advance except for frying chicken
